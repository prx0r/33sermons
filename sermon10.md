# Nibbāna – The Mind Stilled: Sermon 10

**By Bhikkhu K. Ñāṇananda**

---

> *Etaṃ santaṃ, etaṃ paṇītaṃ, yadidaṃ sabbasaṅkhārasamatho sabbūpadhipaṭinissaggo taṇhakkhayo virāgo nirodho nibbānaṃ.*

---

## The Teaching

### The Dumb Show Deepened

By way of analogy we brought in the simile of a mime or a dumb show. What characterizes that kind of drama is the comic nature of the acts which depict scenes suggestive of animate or inanimate objects not actually present on the stage. A meditator becomes aware, while attending to his postures, that he is merely enacting a dumb show. He comes to understand how far name is dependent on form, and the four elements appear to him as empty.

In the Satipaṭṭhānasutta we find the following instruction in regard to the keeping of postures: *Yathā yathā vā pan'assa kāyo paṇihito hoti tathā tathā naṃ pajānāti* — "in whatever way his body is disposed, so he understands it." This is suggestive of the attempt of a spectator to understand the mimicry of an actor in a pantomime. While attending to one's postures one feels as if one is watching a one-man dumb show.

When one sees name as merely a shadow of form, and form as a shadow of name, one realizes the hollowness of both. The understanding of this mutual dependence reveals that neither has any essence. This realization is the beginning of insight.

---

### The Three Doors to Liberation

It is by means of the three doors to liberation — the signless (*animitta*), the desireless (*appaṇihita*), and the void (*suññata*) — that the drama of existence comes to an end. The perception of impermanence is the main contributory factor for the cessation of this drama.

The signless liberation (*animitta vimokkha*) is so called because the mind does not grasp at signs. It does not pick out features from experience to construct a world. The desireless liberation (*appaṇihita vimokkha*) is so called because the mind does not incline towards any object with craving. And the void liberation (*suññata vimokkha*) is so called because all things are seen as empty of self.

These three are not three different destinations but three aspects of the same liberation. When the mind stops picking out signs, it stops desiring. When it stops desiring, it sees emptiness. When it sees emptiness, it stops picking out signs.

---

### The Posture and the Pantomime

The instruction to understand the body "in whatever way it is disposed" is a meditation on the relativity of name and form. Each posture — walking, standing, sitting, lying down — is a configuration of the four elements. The "name" aspect is the knowing of the posture. The "form" aspect is the physical configuration itself.

One does not exist without the other. The posture is known because name knows it. Name knows it because the posture is there. This mutual dependence shows the emptiness of both. The meditator sees through the dumb show.

## Plain-English Notes

### The Three Doors

| Liberation | Pali | What it means |
|-----------|------|---------------|
| Signless | Animitta | Mind doesn't grasp at signs/features |
| Desireless | Appaṇihita | Mind doesn't incline with craving |
| Void | Suññata | All seen as empty of self |

These are not sequential stages but aspects of a single release. When you truly see impermanence, all three open at once.

### Name as Shadow of Form

Name is like a shadow cast by form. It has no independent existence. But form, in turn, is known only through name. They lean on each other like the two bundles of reeds. Neither is substantial. The meditator sees this in the simple act of attending to a posture.

---

## Meditations

### The Posture Pantomime

Take a posture — sitting, standing, or walking. Now attend to it as a mime would. Notice: you are "acting" the posture. The body is disposed in a certain way. Name is tracking it. But what is "the posture" independent of name? Can you find it?

Don't try to answer. Just keep attending: "in whatever way the body is disposed, so I understand it." Not adding anything, not subtracting anything. Just watching the dumb show.

**The logic this reveals:** The posture is empty. It's a configuration of elements that name tracks. Neither is substantial. The drama is seen as drama.
